Predicting the Quality of Water Based On Given Parameters From kaggle: https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/tfug-mysuru-water-quality-prediction/data by Aditya Subramanian Introduction As climate change and pollution become rampant in the 21st century, 3rd world countries and poorer communities face issues with obtaining what should be a basic human right: drinkable water. As of today, more than 750 million individuals all around the world do not have access to pure, drinkable water. To put this into perspective, that number is proportional to a tenth of the total population!() In order to understand the effect of water pollution (whether caused by the process of industrialization or not) on both humanity and nature, it is necessary to develop a machine learning algorithm, which given the features of any given body of water, is able to determine whether that body is drinkable or not. This project attempts to provide a machine learning algorithm which is capable of determining whether a body of water is drinkable based on the similarities between the target and other bodies of water in the training set. In this way, factors which contribute to the drinkable water crisis can be isolated in order to determine causal relationships.

#Function Definitions for Plotting Correlations
def bar_plot_func(dataframe, x_column, y_column, title, x_label, y_label,bool=False):
    output_list = []
    input_list = dataframe[x_column].unique()
    for input in input_list:
        average= dataframe.groupby(x_column).get_group(input)[y_column].mean()
        output_list.append(average)
    for i in range(len(input_list)):
        input_list[i] = (input_list[i].astype(str))
        # print(type(input_list[i]))
    # if bool:
    #     input_list = ["No", "Yes"]
    fig1, ax1 = plt.subplots()
    ax1.bar(input_list, output_list)
    ax1.set_title(title)
    ax1.set_xlabel(x_label)
    ax1.set_ylabel(y_label)
